Key things to know
Transitioning from the old structure
Some of you have been given titles or pay that reflected our old structure, one that no longer serves where Bak'd is going. If your current pay is higher than where your level lands, you will be given a 3, 6, or 9 month timeline to level up to match it. If you have not reached that level by the end of your timeline, your pay will move to reflect the level you are at. We want to give everyone a fair runway, but the expectation is that you use it. You will receive a second email from JALANDY if this applies to you.
Self-assessment first
You decide which level you think you're at and request that test. If you pass, that's your level. If you don't, you test for the level below. When in doubt, test higher, the worst that happens is you drop down a level.
Two parts to every test
A knowledge test (80%+ to pass) and a practical test. You need to pass both. If you fail either part, you have not passed that level.
Test results
You'll receive your test score after completing the knowledge test. Both managers reviewing your test will share results with you.
Cross-training
You can level up in FOH, BOH, and Markets. You can test for FOH and BOH in the same window, just book separate sessions for each. There's also a small bonus for cross-training in additional departments. More details coming in Phase 2.
New hires
Anyone hired after the rollout starts at Level 0 during probation. At their first assessment round, they self-assess and test for whatever level they believe they're at, same process as everyone else.
Hours requirement (FOH + BOH)
To advance levels in FOH or BOH, you need 320+ total hours with the company. This is your total hours worked, not department-specific. You can check your hours anytime in Square Teams. Most relevant for full-time staff who want to grow with Bak'd, optional for part-time.
Levels are company-wide
Your level follows you across all Bak'd locations. The structure is consistent whether you're at New West or Bak'd 2.
Growth opportunities
Assistant Manager roles require Level 4 (Mastered) in both FOH and BOH. As Bak'd grows, those roles are created first from within.

Front of house levels

Barista skills, POS, customer experience, and coffee mastery.

Level 0 — Foundation
  • Knowledge test: Bak'd story, allergens, ingredients, all menu items
  • Delivery apps (Uber, Skip, DoorDash), packaging, bulk pricing, phone procedure, labelling, cleaning
  • POS basics
  • Barista basics
Level 1 — Novice
  • Knowledge test: dialling in coffee shots, over vs under extraction + how to fix
  • Troubleshooting guide without referring to it
  • Troubleshooting POS
  • Definitions of bar lingo
  • Practical: pulling shots, bar cleaning
Level 2 — Skilled
  • Knowledge test: bar lingo + tools identification, proper steamed milk (aeration, rolling, binding)
  • Practical: latte art (rosetta or equivalent)
  • Demonstrate proper steaming: lil foam, BAK'D foam, lotta foam
  • Little to no excess milk left in pitcher
  • Proper sequencing with extracting espresso and steaming milk
  • Clean and tidy workspace + steam wand (fail vs pass)
Level 3 — Advanced
  • Knowledge test: ice machine cleaning, grinder cleaning with rice, shot troubleshooting (channeling, slow pulls, yield risks, decaf specifics)
  • Practical: blind grinder dial-in (intentionally set off)
  • Execution of stacked latte art
  • Using Manual button, auto fail if shot is outside company standard (25 seconds)
  • Blind taste test: under, over, and well-extracted espresso
  • Extensive grinder dialling in + grinder troubleshoot
Level 4 — Mastered
  • Knowledge test: how to explain rosetta + stack design execution, training approach for staff serving bad shots, espresso machine/grinder troubleshoot steps
  • Latte art on a macchiato, assessor calls which style on the day
  • Blind fix of espresso machine: identify what's been changed and correct it
Training practical, pass/fail checklist
Caught the intentional mistake made during the training session
Trainee completed all steps without being prompted by assessor
Every shot was weighed and served with correct yield
Corrected the trainee's tamping technique (wrist straight, even, level, correct dose)
Aeration, rolling, and binding all verbally explained or demonstrated
Milk waste was addressed
Trainee was observed sequencing and multitasking, not making one drink at a time
Pouring technique coached, trainee set up for success, can identify what's going wrong
Workspace stayed clean throughout, spills addressed in real time
Both managers score independently. Minimum 7 of 9 checkpoints required to pass.

Back of house levels

Cookie production, baking, decorating, and packaging mastery.

Level 0 — Foundation
  • Knowledge test
  • POS basics
  • Production basics: measuring, mixing, balling, tray labelling, scooping fillings
Level 1 — Novice
  • Knowledge test: average batch yield (~200), 5 ingredients + mixing order, FIFO, doughs with fillings + patty weight range (4.2 to 4.5oz), shortage protocol
  • Recipe recognition (when numbers look off)
  • Practical: balling speed test, bake assist practical
Level 2 — Skilled
  • Knowledge test: which doughs need pushing down (Matcha, Classic, OG), average bake time (12 min), tray rotation at 6 min, rack swap pairs (1&3, 2&4), wet dough fix
  • Practical: decorating, troubleshooting mixing, baking practical
  • Packaging practical: printing labels (markets & orders)
Level 3 — Advanced
  • Knowledge test: dough cutoff for production (~150), timer add-ons for underbaked cookies (1:30 to 3 min), production priority order (Bisc first, OG last), handling partially done trays, spreading issues
  • Practical: lead baking, to-do list prioritization
  • Training production practical test
  • Troubleshoot baking scenarios
Level 4 — Mastered
  • Knowledge test: encompasses random questions from Levels 1 to 3
  • Practical: training baking + packaging
  • Staggered baking practical test
  • Bake high volumes using staggering method

How to level up

The process, the timeline, and what you need to do.

Step by step
Step 1
Decide which level you think you're at. When in doubt, test higher, the worst that happens is you drop down a level.
Step 2
Book your test slot using the booking link (releasing mid-April). Separate bookings needed for FOH and BOH if testing both.
Step 3
Take the knowledge test. You need 80%+ to pass. Your score will be shared with you after. Fail = you test for the level below.
Step 4
Complete the practical test. Graded by both managers independently.
Step 5
Pass both = you're placed at that level. More info on what that means for pay is coming in Phase 2.

Rules to know
To pass a level
Score 80%+ on the knowledge test AND pass the practical. Both are required. Failing either part means you have not passed that level.
If you fail after the initial rollout
You can rebook within available slots in the makeup week (subject to availability). If no slots remain, you'll test at the next testing period. Study hard and come prepared, slots fill up.
Checking your hours
You can view your total hours anytime in Square Teams. The 320-hour requirement is based on total hours with the company, not department-specific.
Markets
No hours requirement. Test when you feel ready during the testing window.
New hires
Start at Level 0 during probation. At their first assessment round, self-assess and test for whatever level they believe they're at.
Scheduling your test
Your manager will ensure they're available during the testing window. Use the booking link to lock in your slot.
Levels are company-wide
Your level is consistent across all Bak'd locations.

Sample test questions

Use these to study, not just memorize. Some answers are revealed on click. Others you'll need to work out yourself.

Tap "show answer" where available. Not every question has one, that's intentional.
FOH Knowledge Level 1
An espresso shot pulls in 15 seconds. Is it over- or under-extracted, and what do you adjust?
show answer
Under-extracted. Adjust grind finer or increase dose.
What does over-extracted espresso taste like, and what causes it?
show answer
Bitter. Shot pulled too long or grind too fine.
Walk me through troubleshooting the POS when it's not working.
Define three pieces of bar lingo we use at Bak'd.
FOH Knowledge Level 2
What are the 3 things that promote proper steamed milk?
show answer
Aeration, rolling, binding.
What is the correct order from start to finish when steaming milk?
show answer
Measure, purge, aerate, roll, purge, bind.
You steam milk and the end product is grainy and clumpy. What caused this?
What does properly steamed milk look like?
FOH Practical Level 2
Demonstrate steaming for lil foam, BAK'D foam, and lotta foam. One will be called randomly on the day.
Free-pour a rosetta design. Pass/fail.
Clean and tidy your workspace + steam wand at the end of your session. Pass/fail.
FOH Knowledge Level 3
Walk me through how to properly clean the ice machine.
show answer
Power off, remove all ice/water, remove slats and grate, sanitize, reinstall grate and slats, power on, dispose of first 5 batches of ice.
Your shot is pulling slow. What are the two causes, and which do you fix first?
show answer
Improper dose or grind size. Adjust dose first, then grind size.
What is the purpose of rice when cleaning the grinder, and in what order do you do it?
What happens if you pull a shot with a crack in the puck?
Why can decaf run fast?
FOH Practical Level 3
Grinder will be intentionally set off. Diagnose and fix it without any reference materials.
Use the Manual button to pull a shot. Auto fail if outside 25 seconds.
Blind taste test: identify under-extracted, over-extracted, and well-extracted espresso.
FOH Practical Level 4 — Mastered
Latte art on a macchiato, assessor calls the style on the day (rosetta or stack).
Blind fix of espresso machine, something will be changed. Identify it and correct it.
Training practical: train a lower-level barista. Assessors score 9 checkpoints independently. Minimum 7 of 9 to pass. You won't know which checkpoints are being watched.
BOH Knowledge Level 1
What are the 5 ingredients in each cookie batch, in mixing order?
show answer
1. Butter, 2. Sugars, 3. Eggs, 4. Flour/dry ingredients, 5. Toppings/mix-ins.
Walk me through the full shortage protocol.
show answer
1. Flag to manager. 2. Get approval to go to Save-On. If not approved, plastic wrap each bowl with flavour, date, amount, initials. 3. Communicate to manager + production team. 4. Manager may adjust batch yield.
What does FIFO mean and why does it matter?
What is the patty weight range for doughs that have fillings?
BOH Knowledge Level 2
What's the average bake time, and when do you rotate trays?
show answer
12 minutes total. Rotate after the first 6 minutes.
Which doughs need pushing down when prepping trays?
Which racks are swapped when rotating during a bake?
The dough is too wet mid-mix. What do you do?
BOH Knowledge Level 3
What's the production priority order?
show answer
Bisc first, then Oreo, Matcha, OG last.
A cookie is still underbaked after 12 minutes. How much extra time do you add?
What's the dough cutoff number before it hits the production to-do list?
A cookie is spreading too much when it comes out. What do you do first?
A few cookies on the same tray aren't done but the rest are. What do you do?
Markets Knowledge
You arrive and the Square reader won't connect. Walk me through your steps.
It's windy and your tent is starting to lift. What do you do, in what order?
You've sold out of two flavours 45 minutes before close. What do you do?

FAQ

Real questions, answered.

When does the booking link go live? ▼
Mid-April. Watch #serious-business for the announcement. The test window itself runs May 11 to 24.
Where can I find my total hours? ▼
You can check your total hours anytime in Square Teams.
Where can I find study materials? ▼
All study guides and SOPs are here: Bak'd Study Guides and SOPs. FOH materials are being revamped and will be available end of next week. A Slack message will go out when they are live.
I'm not sure if I'm a Level 2 or 3, what should I do? ▼
When in doubt, test higher. The worst that happens is you drop down a level and get placed there instead. The test is the most reliable way to find out where you actually sit.
What if my current pay is higher than where my level lands? ▼
If your current pay reflects a previous role or title from our old structure, you'll be given a 3, 6, or 9 month timeline to level up to match it. If you haven't reached that level by the end of your timeline, your pay will move to reflect the level you are at. Anyone this applies to will receive a second email from JALANDY.
What if I fail a test? ▼
You'll test for the level below instead. After the initial rollout, you can rebook within whatever slots are still available in the makeup week (week of May 25), or book for the next testing period. Slots fill up, study hard and come prepared.
What if there are no slots left in the makeup week? ▼
If the makeup week is fully booked, you'll test at the next testing period. The best way to avoid this is to book early and come prepared the first time.
What if I pass the knowledge test but fail the practical, or vice versa? ▼
You need to pass both. If you fail either part, you haven't passed that level.
Will I get my test score back? ▼
Yes, your knowledge test score will be shared with you after you complete it.
Do I have to test in order, starting at Level 0? ▼
Nope. You self-assess and test for the level you think you're at. When in doubt, test higher. The test validates your existing skill, it's not a school where you start at the beginning.
Do FOH hours count toward a BOH test, and vice versa? ▼
Yes. The 320 hours is your total hours worked with the company, not department-specific. You can check this in Square Teams.
I work in both FOH and BOH, how does that work? ▼
You can test for both in the same window, just book separate sessions for each department.
Is there a bonus for cross-training? ▼
Yes, there's a small cross-training bonus for qualifying in additional departments. Full details are coming in Phase 2 around May 27.
Do I need a certain number of hours to test for Markets? ▼
No hours requirement for markets. Test when you feel ready during the testing window.
Who grades my test? ▼
Both managers review your test independently. For practicals, they score separately and compare. Results are shared with you after.
Will a manager be available to test me? ▼
Yes, your manager will ensure they're available during the testing window. Book your slot through the booking link when it goes live in mid-April.
What if I can't make the May 11 to 24 window? ▼
There's a makeup window the week of May 25, subject to availability. If no slots remain, you'll test at the next testing period. Flag it to your manager as early as possible.
I was just hired, what happens to me? ▼
You start at Level 0 during your probation period. At your first assessment round, you self-assess and test for whatever level you think you're at, same process as everyone else.
If I transfer to Bak'd 2, does my level carry over? ▼
Yes. Levels are consistent across all Bak'd locations. Your level follows you.
What does Mastered (Level 4) open up for me? ▼
Mastered in both FOH and BOH is the requirement for Assistant Manager roles. Those roles are created as Bak'd grows, Bak'd 2, Bak'd 3, and beyond. If you're aiming for leadership, this is the path.
Can I see the actual test in advance? ▼
No, questions are kept private so tests stay fair. The sample questions in this guide are a great study resource. There are also multiple versions of each test.
What if I think my result is wrong or unfair? ▼
Email Jess and Andy immediately at jess@bakdcookies.ca and andy@bakdcookies.ca. If there's a genuine concern about how a test was graded, it will be reviewed.
